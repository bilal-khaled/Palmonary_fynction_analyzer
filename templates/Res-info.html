Restrictive lung disease

Chest Wall Disease




Chest wall diseases can be related to restrictive lung diseases because they can affect the ability of the lungs to expand and contract during breathing, leading to a reduction in lung volume and decreased lung function. The chest wall is the bony and muscular structure that surrounds the lungs and helps to protect them. If the chest wall is weakened or deformed, it can interfere with the mechanics of breathing and result in a restrictive lung disease.


Neuro-muscular Disease


Neuromuscular diseases (NMDs) can be related to restrictive lung diseases because they can affect the function of the muscles involved in breathing, such as the diaphragm and intercostal muscles. The diaphragm is the primary muscle responsible for breathing, and the intercostal muscles help to expand and contract the rib cage during breathing. If the muscles involved in breathing are weakened or damaged, it can lead to a reduction in lung volume and decreased lung function, which can result in a restrictive lung disease.
