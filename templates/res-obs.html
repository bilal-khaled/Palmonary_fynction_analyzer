Restrictive lung disease

A type of respiratory condition that is characterized by a reduction in lung volume, making it difficult to fully expand the lungs and breathe in enough air. This can result in shortness of breath, chest pain, rapid shallow breathing, dry cough, and fatigue. The most common causes include lung tissue scarring, chest wall deformities, neuromuscular diseases, and obesity. Other causes may include radiation therapy, certain medications, and autoimmune diseases

Obstructive lung disease

A type of respiratory condition that is characterized by airflow limitation due to narrowing of the airways. This can make it difficult to breathe, particularly during exhalation, wheezing, coughing, and chest tightness. Obstructive lung disease can be caused by a variety of factors, including smoking, air pollution, genetic factors, and occupational exposures.
