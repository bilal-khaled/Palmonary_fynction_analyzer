Bronchodilator Test


A test used to diagnose and monitor obstructive lung diseases such as asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD). It involves measuring lung function before and after inhaling a medication called a bronchodilator, which relaxes and opens up the airways in the lungs.


  1. Preparation: You will be asked to refrain from using certain medications that could affect the results of the test, such as bronchodilators, for a period of time before the test. You may also be asked to avoid consuming caffeine or other stimulants before the test.
  2. Lung function tests: your first spirometry measurements will be applied.
  3. Bronchodilator inhalation: You will then inhale a measured dose of a bronchodilator medication such as albuterol or salbutamol, using a nebulizer or inhaler. The medication will relax and open up the airways in your lungs, making it easier to breathe.
  4. Repeat lung function tests: After waiting a few minutes for the medication to take effect, you will be asked to perform the same breathing tests again to measure any improvements in lung function. The results will be compared to your baseline lung function measurements taken before the inhalation.

It's important to follow your healthcare provider's instructions and ask any questions you may have before, during, or after the test.
